The National Library

National library is a library established by the federal government and it is maintained by national resources through tax payers’ money. National library strives principally to collect and preserve the nation’s literature though it strives to be much as international in the range of its collections as possible.

National library is to other libraries what the central bank is to other banks in any country. It is therefore referred to as an apex organization for libraries in its country. For example, the national library of Nigeria library of Nigeria has branches in many states of the federation namely, Kwara, Ondo, Ogun, Kaduna, and Oyo states. Examples of national library include; National Library of Nigeria, National Library of Ghana, National Library of Scotland etc.

Functions of National Library
  1. It receives, by law through legal deposit obligations, copies of all publications, books and periodicals.
  2. It has the responsibility of publishing a National Bibliography of its country.
  3. It serves as the National Agency for the issuance of ISBN (International Standard Book Number) and it serves as the National Serials Data Centre (NSDC) for ISSN (International Standard Serial Number) of its nation
  4. It is responsible for the control of the National Union Catalogue and National Union List of Serials (NULOS) of its country.
  5. It also acquires foreign documents of national important and interest.
