The Internet and World Wide Web

Although the internet and the World Wide Web (www) are used interchangeably, they are not the same. Webopedia, defines the internet as ‘a global network of millions of computers’ that communicate with each other. It is a networking infrastructure in which any computer can communicate with any other computer as long as they are both connected to the internet. Information that travels over the internet does so via a variety of language known as protocols.

Online services like American Online, CompuServe and MSN provide access to the internet. Commercial Internet Service Providers (ISP) also facilitates access to the facility.

World Wide Web is a system of internet servers that support specially formatted documents. The documents are formatted in a markup language called HTML (Hypertext Markup Language) that support links to other documents as well as graphics, audio, and video files. This means you can jump from one document to another simply by clicking on hot spots. Not all Internet servers are part of the World Wide Web

There are several applications called Web browsers that make it easy to access the World Wide Web; Two of the most popular being Firefox and Microsoft’s Internet Explorer. The web is just one of the ways whereby information can be disseminated over the Internet. There are different forms of library that rely solely on electronics in accessing information from them. These include virtual library, electronic library and digital library.
