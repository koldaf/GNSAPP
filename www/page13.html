Applications of ICT to Library

Information and communication Technology is the application of micro-electric based computing and telecommunications technology to library and information work. Generally, it is the combination of computers and telecommunication in handling and disseminating information. ICT in the context of library is used for the creation, acquisition, storage, retrieval and dissemination of information. The era of information and communication technology ushered in the use of electronics in library services. As a result, information is accessed through internet. The internet is a huge information resource, some of which can be used for academic work.

The aim of this chapter is to draw the attentions of students to selected internet resources that they can consult for academic work in the university. It needs to be pointed out that the internet can be used for several other purposes apart from academic work. Asefeh & Nosrat (2007)) submitted that when the user is aware of one helpful resource, it will usually lead to greater use of that resource.

Scholarly information, however, is what is required for learning, teaching, research and extension activities in a university and this is the major reason why this chapter is concentrating on how to access and retrieve this type of information from the internet. Supporting Tsakonas and Papatheodorou (2006) that the transaction from print to electronic medium, apart from resulting in a growth of electronic information, has provided users with new tools and applications for information seeking and retrieval, scholars must know the resources available and develop search techniques.
