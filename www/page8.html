The School Library

School libraries are libraries in the nursery, primary, and Secondary schools. The main purpose of a school library is to encourage reading habit and to develop studentsâ€™ ability to learn from books without a teacher (Olanlokan and Salisu, 1993). An Example of a School Library

In a school library, learning resources such as books and audio-visual materials are systematically collected and organized for the use of the students and teachers. The new educational trend requires the utilization of non-book resources such as flash cards, slides, filmstrips, records and computer in the teaching and learning process. This is because emphasis is no longer on the subject matter but on the learner.
A school Library performs the following functions, among others;

  1. It provides relevant materials to enable students and pupils improve on their academic standard
  2. It makes materials available to complement the teaching activities of teachers and also to enhance the achievement of the goals and objectives of the school.
  3. It put in place activities and resources capable of developing and sustaining the habit and enjoyment of reading and learning independently in the pupils/students.
  4. It supports students/pupils in learning and acquiring practical skills required to evaluate information and become sensitive to the modes of communication within the company.
  5. The school library assists students/pupils to recognize information books and other library resources.
