The Public Library

Public libraries are those which are established by government to serve all categorize of people in the society. Public libraries can be owned by federal government, state government, local government, or a community. These libraries are usually located at the state or local government Headquarters but can also have branches sited in other parts of the districts, wards and communities as the case may be.

A library that is owned or established, by individuals or corporate/non-governmental body is termed a private library. Such library is not usually open to every user but can only be used through the consent and approval of the owners. However, such library becomes public when it is made open for community or general public use. Examples are Late Dr. Nnamadi Azikwe library, Late Chief Adeniran Ogunsanya Library, Lagos and Afolayan Memorial library, Iludun-Oro, Kwara state. Such library provides relevant materials to friends, members of the owner family and the community.

Functions of Public Library
  1. A public library stock books and other resources on diverse human knowledge for the use of members of the community where the library is located.
  2. A public library often has a section meant for children and another part for adult users. Public utilities such as conference facilities, restaurant, caf√©, and viewing rooms and media section may be found in a large public library.
  3. Government-owned public library promotes mobile library services for people in the rural communities by using mobile vehicles to move library materials to the people.
  4. It preserves the cultural monuments and even artifacts of a people. It also stocks the indigenous literature and audiovisual materials above the people.
